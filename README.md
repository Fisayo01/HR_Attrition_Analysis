# HR Attrition Analysis
## Project Overview
This project focuses on analyzing employee attrition patterns using Power BI. The goal was to identify the major factors contributing to employee turnover and present actionable insights through an interactive dashboard.

The dashboard provides HR teams and business stakeholders with a clear understanding of workforce trends, employee behavior, and retention challenges.

## Objectives

The main objectives of this project were to:

- Analyze employee attrition trends
- Identify key drivers of employee turnover
- Explore relationships between overtime, job satisfaction, age, department, and attrition
- Build an interactive dashboard for business decision-making
- Practice data storytelling and dashboard design in Power BI


## Tools & Technologies Used
- Power BI
- Power Query
- DAX (Data Analysis Expressions)
- Microsoft Excel / CSV Dataset

## Dataset Information
The dataset used contains HR employee records, including:
- Employee demographics
- Department information
- Job roles
- Monthly income
- Job satisfaction
- Overtime status
- Years at company
- Attrition status

## Dashboard Features
The dashboard includes:
- KPI Cards
- Attrition Analysis by Department
- Overtime vs Attrition Analysis
- Job Satisfaction Analysis
- Employee Age Group Analysis
- Distance from Home Analysis
- Gender Distribution
- Interactive Filters/Slicers

## Key Insights
1. Overtime Strongly Influences Attrition
Employees working overtime showed significantly higher attrition levels, suggesting workload pressure and work-life imbalance are major retention challenges.

2. Early Tenure Employees Leave More
Employees within their first few years at the company experienced the highest attrition, indicating possible onboarding and engagement issues.

3. Employees Aged 26–35 Have the Highest Attrition
The organization’s core working-age employees showed the highest turnover rate.

4. Research & Development and Sales Departments Recorded High Attrition
These departments may require improved employee engagement and workload management strategies.

5. Job Satisfaction Impacts Retention
Lower job satisfaction levels were associated with increased employee attrition.
